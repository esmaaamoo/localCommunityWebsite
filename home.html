ABOUT US

Lenggong is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its archaeological significance and natural beauty. The Lenggong Valley offers a glimpse into prehistoric life, with many ancient tools and artifacts uncovered in the area. The valley is also home to beautiful landscapes, making it a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Lenggong, a historical town in Malaysia, was named after a man who cut down a tree, resulting in a slanting log. The name has evolved over time, and it is believed to be the birthplace of the first man in Malaysia. Today the town houses exhibits showcasing the lives of people during the Palaeolithic era. Lenggong was recognized as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 2012, making it the 4th World Heritage Site in Malaysia and the 953rd globally. Notable prehistoric evidence includes the human skeleton of the Perak Man, the most complete in Southeast Asia, providing information about lifestyle, beliefs, diet, technology, race, gender, and disease during the Paleolithic Era.
